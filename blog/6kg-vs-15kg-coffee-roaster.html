6kg vs 15kg Coffee Roaster: Which is Right for Your Roastery?

📅 June 2024⏱ 6 min read✍️ Yoshan Roasting Team🏷 Comparison

You've outgrown your 1–3kg starter roaster and you're ready to scale. The question every serious roastery owner faces: should you jump to a 6kg machine or go straight to 15kg? This comparison breaks it down with real numbers.

The Core Difference: Capacity and Flexibility

A 6kg roaster (like the Yoshan YS-6KG) handles 3.5–7kg per batch at 18–28kg/hour output. A 15kg machine (like the YS-15KG or SD-15KG Pro) handles 1–15kg per batch at 40–80kg/hour output.

The 15kg machine isn't just "bigger" — it gives you batch flexibility. You can run 3kg batches for single-origin specialty lots and switch to 15kg batches for your commercial blends on the same machine. The 6kg machine maxes out at 7kg.

When to Choose the 6kg Machine

  • You roast fewer than 100kg per day
  • Your focus is specialty/single-origin with frequent small batches
  • Space is limited (the 6kg has a smaller footprint)
  • You want to minimize upfront investment while you grow your customer base
  • You're roasting for 1–3 cafés or a small subscription list

Real-world example: A specialty café roasting 60kg/day of various single-origins — the 6kg machine (running at 5–7kg batches) handles this comfortably in a 4–5 hour roasting session, leaving time for cooling, bagging, and quality control.

When to Choose the 15kg Machine

  • You're supplying 5+ cafés or running a wholesale business
  • You roast 100–400kg per day or plan to reach this within 12 months
  • You need batch flexibility — both small specialty lots AND large commercial batches
  • You want room to grow without buying a second machine
  • You're building a brand that will scale to retail/online sales

Real-world example: A regional roastery supplying 8 café accounts needs to roast 180kg/day. The 15kg machine (running 12–15kg batches) handles this in a 5-hour window — the 6kg would require 8+ hours of continuous roasting.

The Price Gap is Smaller Than You Think

The Yoshan YS-15KG is only $3,500 more than the YS-6KG ($10,999 vs $7,499). Spread over the machine's 10+ year lifespan, that's less than $30/month in additional cost — while more than doubling your hourly output capacity.

If there's any realistic chance you'll need 15kg capacity within 2 years, the math almost always favors buying the 15kg now rather than upgrading later (which means buying a second machine, paying for shipping twice, and potentially paying for installation twice).

Our Recommendation

Choose the 6kg if you're a specialty-focused café or small roastery with under 100kg/day needs and a tight budget.

Choose the 15kg if you're running or planning to run a wholesale roastery, supplying multiple accounts, or expect to scale past 100kg/day within 1–2 years.

When in doubt: go 15kg. The extra $3,500 buys you double the capacity and years of headroom.
