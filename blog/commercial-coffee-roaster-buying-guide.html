The Complete Commercial Coffee Roaster Buying Guide 2024

📅 June 2024⏱ 10 min read✍️ Yoshan Roasting Team🏷 Buying Guide

Buying a commercial coffee roaster is a major investment — one that will shape your coffee quality, operational efficiency, and business growth for the next decade. This guide covers everything you need to know before making a decision.

1. Define "Commercial" for Your Business

The word "commercial" spans a wide range. In the coffee roasting world, we define commercial machines as anything from 6kg and above — machines built for daily production roasting rather than casual home use. Within the commercial category, there are three distinct tiers:

  • Entry commercial (6–15kg): For specialty roasteries, multi-café suppliers, and growing independent brands. Daily output: 50–400kg.
  • Mid commercial (20–60kg): For regional wholesale roasters and private label brands. Daily output: 200–1,200kg.
  • Industrial (60kg–300kg): For national brands, private label manufacturers, and export-focused factories. Daily output: 500kg+.

2. The 5 Most Important Specs to Compare

① Drum Material — Cast Iron vs. Stainless Steel

Cast iron drums retain heat far better than stainless steel. This means more even heat distribution across the bean mass, better consistency batch-to-batch, and less gas consumption per kg of coffee roasted. All Yoshan YS and SD series use cast iron drums for this reason.

Stainless steel drums are lighter, easier to clean, and heat up faster — making them popular in smaller 1–3kg machines where batch frequency is high. Yoshan's DY series uses 304 food-grade stainless steel.

② Control System — PLC vs. Manual

For any commercial machine above 6kg, we strongly recommend a PLC (Programmable Logic Controller) touchscreen system. Manual control is fine for artisan roasters who want hands-on control — but for commercial production, PLC systems enable:

  • Roast profile recording and automatic playback
  • Consistent batch-to-batch replication
  • Remote monitoring and alarm systems
  • Lower labor requirements (one operator can manage the machine)

Yoshan YS series uses Siemens PLC — the same brand used in European industrial equipment. SD series uses Yoshan's proprietary PLC system with optional Siemens upgrade.

③ Burner Brand and Type

The burner is the heart of a gas-fired roaster. For large commercial and industrial machines, the burner brand matters significantly for efficiency, longevity, and emissions compliance.

Yoshan's SD-20KG Pro and above use Weishaupt burners — the same German brand used by Probat. This is not a coincidence: Weishaupt burners offer precise gas modulation, low emissions, and decades of reliability in industrial applications.

④ Temperature Control Precision

Specialty coffee requires tight temperature control — ideally within ±1°C or better during the roast. Industrial machines should be even tighter. Yoshan's full-automatic SD series achieves ±0.1°C precision during the temperature-chasing process — a benchmark that rivals any European machine.

⑤ After-Sales Support and Warranty

A commercial roaster will run for 10–15 years. The after-sales support infrastructure matters as much as the machine specs. Ask any potential supplier:

  • What is the standard warranty, and what does it cover?
  • How quickly can you get replacement parts?
  • Do you have technicians who can support installation and commissioning?
  • Is there a technical support hotline?

Yoshan provides a 1-year standard warranty (extendable to 3 years), a 24-hour after-sales team, and a CEO hotline for critical issues. For Southeast Asia buyers, free on-site commissioning engineers are provided for SD-20Pro and above.

4. Total Cost of Ownership — Not Just Purchase Price

The sticker price is only part of the story. Factor in:

  • Gas consumption: A 15kg machine consumes ~4.5m³/hour of natural gas. Over 8 hours of daily roasting, that's significant. More efficient burners (like Weishaupt) reduce this cost.
  • Accessories: A destoner ($1,299–$4,469), afterburner ($4,300–$9,500), and auto loader ($1,199–$2,950) are often necessary — especially if your local authority requires smoke emission controls.
  • Installation: Gas line connection, electrical work, ventilation, and foundation may add $2,000–$10,000 depending on your location.
  • Extended warranty: Budget for 2–3 year warranty coverage. Yoshan's extended warranty costs $299 (2yr) or $799 (3yr) for DY series; $699/year for YS/SD.

Bundle and save: Accessories purchased together with a Yoshan roaster receive a 20% discount. Plan your full setup before ordering.

5. Questions to Ask Any Supplier Before Buying

  1. Can I visit your factory or request a virtual factory tour?
  2. What is the lead time for my machine?
  3. Can you customize the color and add my logo?
  4. Do you offer an Artisan software integration?
  5. What voltage variants are available for my country?
  6. What is included in the price (auto loader, destoner, afterburner)?
  7. Do you have reference customers in my region I can contact?
